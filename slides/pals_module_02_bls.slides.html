Objectives

You will be able to

Recognition of Arrest

The 10-Second Window

Action sequence

  1. Verify scene safety.
  2. Tap and shout — assess responsiveness.
  3. Shout for help / activate ERS / get AED.
  4. Simultaneously check breathing and pulse for ≤10 s.
  5. If no pulse or HR < 60 with poor perfusion → CPR.

Pulse check site

  • Infant (<1 y): brachial
  • Child: carotid or femoral
  • If uncertain after 10 s → assume cardiac arrest and start CPR.

AED Use

Defibrillate Early When Indicated

Pad selection

  • <8 years / <25 kg: pediatric pads or attenuator.
  • ≥8 years: adult pads.
  • If pediatric unavailable → adult pads acceptable, avoid touching.

Workflow

  1. Power on AED — follow voice prompts.
  2. Place pads (anterior-anterior or A-P).
  3. Clear, analyze, shock if advised.
  4. Resume CPR immediately post-shock — no pulse check.

Clinical Pearl

"Push hard, push fast, don't lean, count it out loud."

Compression fraction is the single strongest predictor of survival. Coach your team aloud: rate, depth, recoil, switch — every two minutes, every time.

Summary

Key Take-Aways

  • Recognize → simultaneous breathing + pulse ≤10 s → CPR.
  • Rate 100-120 · Depth ≥4 cm infant, ≥5 cm child · Full recoil · ≥80% fraction.
  • Ratio: 30:2 (1-rescuer) / 15:2 (2-rescuer infant or child).
  • AED: pediatric pads < 8 y or < 25 kg.
  • Switch every 2 minutes. Resume compressions immediately after shock.
