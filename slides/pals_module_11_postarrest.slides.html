Post-Cardiac Arrest Care

Optimize oxygenation, perfusion, temperature, and neuro outcome

Objectives

  • Apply post-ROSC oxygen and ventilation targets.
  • Manage hemodynamics with appropriate vasoactives and fluids.
  • Deliver targeted temperature management and avoid fever.
  • Coordinate transport, PICU handoff, and family communication.

Oxygenation & Ventilation

Avoid Hyperoxia and Hyperventilation

Oxygenation

  • Titrate FiO₂ to SpO₂ 94-99%
  • Wean from 100% as soon as ABG / SpO₂ allow
  • Hyperoxia worsens reperfusion injury

Ventilation

  • Target PaCO₂ 35-45 mmHg
  • Use ETCO₂ continuously
  • Permissive hypercapnia only for ARDS / specific contexts
  • Hypocapnia ↓ cerebral perfusion — avoid

Hemodynamic Support

Maintain Mean Perfusion

  • Targets: SBP ≥ 5th percentile for age; MAP guided by age-specific norms.
  • Start with isotonic bolus 10 mL/kg if hypovolemic; reassess.
  • Vasoactive: epinephrine, norepinephrine, dopamine, or milrinone — match to cardiac vs vascular failure.
  • Consider central + arterial line for invasive monitoring (gives DBP feedback).
  • Echocardiogram early — look for myocardial dysfunction, effusion, congenital lesions.
  • Treat ongoing dysrhythmia; correct electrolytes (K⁺, Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺).

Targeted Temperature Management

Avoid Fever — Active Normothermia

  • Continuous core temperature monitoring (bladder/esophageal).
  • Avoid fever > 37.5°C in all comatose post-arrest patients.
  • Options for unresponsive children: targeted normothermia (36-37.5°C) for at least 5 days, or therapeutic hypothermia (32-34°C × 2 days then rewarm to 36-37.5°C × 3 days) — institutional protocol.
  • Treat shivering — sedation, neuromuscular blockade per protocol.

Neuro Monitoring

Anticipate Secondary Injury

Continuous

  • Pupillary check q1 h
  • Pediatric GCS / FOUR score
  • EEG when available — high seizure incidence
  • Glucose Q1-2 h, treat hypo aggressively

Imaging / interventions

  • Head CT / MRI per indication
  • Treat documented seizures with levetiracetam / phenytoin
  • Consult neurology, palliative care for prognosis discussion at 72 h

Communication & Transport

Handoff and Family

  • Closed-loop handoff to PICU: timeline, doses, downtime, complications, family status, code status.
  • Use a structured tool (e.g., SBAR + I-PASS) — write it down.
  • Family presence during resuscitation is supported with appropriate facilitator.
  • Early, honest communication: outcome uncertainty, role of TTM, expected neuro re-evaluation at 72 h.
  • Activate social work, chaplaincy, palliative as needed.

Clinical Pearl

"The first 72 hours decide the next 72 years."

Get oxygen, CO₂, perfusion, and temperature right — and the brain has its best shot. Prognostication before that window is unreliable.
