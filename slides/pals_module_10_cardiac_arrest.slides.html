Core Algorithm

Two Pathways — One 2-Minute Cycle

Cardiac arrest confirmed  →  Start CPR · 100% O₂ · Apply monitor/defib pads · Obtain IV/IO access  →  Check rhythm every 2 minutes

⚡ Shockable VF / Pulseless VT
1

Shock #1: 2 J/kg — asynchronous · resume CPR immediately after

2

CPR × 2 min → rhythm check → Shock #2: 4 J/kg + Epinephrine 0.01 mg/kg

3

CPR × 2 min → rhythm check → Shock #3+: ≥4 J/kg (max 10) + Amiodarone 5 mg/kg

4

Advanced airway · waveform ETCO₂ · epi q3–5 min · treat reversibles

5

Lidocaine 1 mg/kg acceptable if amiodarone unavailable

💙 Non-Shockable Asystole / PEA
1

CPR × 2 min — rate 100–120, depth ≥⅓ AP, full chest recoil

2

Epinephrine 0.01 mg/kg IV/IO q3–5 min — as early as possible in cycle 1

3

Advanced airway → waveform ETCO₂ · 10 breaths/min during CPR

4

Search & treat reversibles — H's & T's including ICH and Heart block

5

Reassess rhythm every 2 min — continue until ROSC or team decision to stop

✅ After every shock

Resume compressions immediately — never pause to check a pulse. Pulse check only at the next 2-minute rhythm-check pause.

2025 Update · Physiology

Physiology-Directed CPR — Measure, Then Act

🩺 Diastolic BP (Art Line)
—

Infants  |  Children: ≥ 30 mmHg

If DBP is below target:

Improve depth · ensure full recoil · rotate compressor · consider earlier epi · rule out tamponade / tension PTX

📈 Waveform ETCO₂
—

Target during CPR  |  ROSC: sudden rise to > 35 mmHg

ETCO₂ interpretations:

Sudden ↑ → ROSC (pause & check pulse)  ·  Sudden ↓ → recurrence or ETT displacement  ·  Persistently low → poor CO or hyperventilation

🔴 The core principle

A 2-minute clock measures time — diastolic BP and ETCO₂ measure perfusion. When arterial access is available, let physiology drive interventions. DBP 14 mmHg + ETCO₂ 9 = compressions need improving, not just a rhythm check.

🔵 Pad placement

⚠️ Epinephrine concentration trap

IV/IO route uses 1:10,000 (0.1 mg/mL) — give 0.1 mL/kg. ET route uses 1:1,000 (1 mg/mL) — give 0.1 mL/kg. These are a 10× difference. Confusing them is a fatal error.

🟢 2025 additions explained

Intracranial hypertension: hyperosmolar therapy (hypertonic saline or mannitol) + brief hyperventilation if herniation suspected.  ·  Heart block: atropine + transcutaneous pacing. Both are now explicitly named reversible causes in 2025 PALS.

2025 Recommendation

ECPR — When ECMO Enters the Algorithm

✅
Reasonable Candidates
  • In-hospital paediatric arrest with a reversible cause
  • ECMO expertise, protocols & trained team immediately available
  • Cardiac surgical populations · myocarditis · refractory VF/pVT
  • Quality CPR maintained throughout pre-cannulation period
  • Activate ECMO team within first 15 minutes if no ROSC
❌
Not Recommended
  • Out-of-hospital arrest (except in specialised programs)
  • Irreversible cause: severe uncontrolled haemorrhage · devastating TBI
  • Prolonged no-flow interval without quality bystander CPR
  • Pre-existing multiple organ failure

⏱️ Timing is everything

Call the ECMO team at 15 minutes of high-quality CPR without ROSC — not 30. Outcome data strongly favours shorter CPR-to-cannulation time. Early activation is a 2025 emphasis change.

Knowledge Check

Tap each card to reveal the answer

Question 1

A child in VF receives their 2nd shock (4 J/kg). What drug do you give next, and when?

Tap to reveal →

Epinephrine 0.01 mg/kg immediately after the 2nd shock, then resume CPR. Amiodarone 5 mg/kg is added after the 3rd shock — not the 2nd.

Question 2

ETCO₂ rises suddenly from 12 to 44 mmHg during CPR. What does this signify?

Tap to reveal →

Probable ROSC. A sustained sudden rise in ETCO₂ to >35 mmHg is the earliest reliable indicator of return of spontaneous circulation — often before a pulse is palpable. Pause compressions and check pulse.

Question 3

Name the two reversible causes added to the H's & T's in the 2025 PALS update.

Tap to reveal →

Intracranial hypertension (ICH) — treat with hyperosmolar therapy ± brief hyperventilation.  ·  Heart block — treat with atropine and transcutaneous pacing. Both are explicit 2025 additions.

Question 4

When should the ECMO team be activated in a child who is not achieving ROSC?

Tap to reveal →

At 15 minutes of high-quality CPR without ROSC in an in-hospital arrest with a reversible cause. 2025 guidelines emphasise early activation — outcome data worsens significantly past 30–40 min CPR-to-cannulation.

Clinical Pearl
"DBP and ETCO₂ tell you whether CPR is working. The clock tells you how long you've been doing it."

A 2-minute clock measures time — arterial diastolic pressure and waveform ETCO₂ measure perfusion. When both are available, trust physiology over the timer. DBP 14 mmHg + ETCO₂ of 9 needs better compressions — not just a rhythm check and a prayer.
