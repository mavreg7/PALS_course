🔵 Open-resource exam — 2025 change

The 2025 AHA guidelines officially endorse an open-resource written exam. You may consult your provider manual, the 2025 guidelines reference card, and any printed notes. This reflects real clinical practice: looking up a dose in a reference is appropriate at the bedside.

AHA / ILCOR 2025 Guidelines

Seven Changes Worth Knowing

1
Simultaneous breathing + pulse check ≥10 s — assess both at the same time rather than sequentially; reduces time to first compression
2
Physiology-directed CPR: use ETCO₂ ≥15–20 mmHg and DBP ≥25 mmHg (infants) / ≥30 mmHg (children) when invasive monitoring is available to guide compression quality
3
Expanded reversible causes (Hs & Ts): added intracranial hypertension and complete heart block to the classic list
4
IV sotalolNEW is now endorsed for refractory SVT that persists after adenosine and a first antiarrhythmic agent — dose 1 mg/kg IV over 5 min
5
Unified chain of survival — in-hospital (IHCA) and out-of-hospital (OHCA) paediatric chains are now integrated into a single continuous loop emphasising community + hospital response
6
ECPRNEW is considered reasonable for IHCA with a reversible cause in centres with ECMO expertise and rapid cannulation capability
7
Open-resource written exam — AHA formally endorses use of printed reference materials during the certification exam, aligning testing with real clinical behaviour

Question 1

What score do you need to pass the PALS written exam, and what resource policy applies in 2025?

Tap to reveal →

≥84% (21 of 25 questions correct). Under 2025 AHA policy the exam is open-resource — provider manual, the 2025 guidelines reference card, and printed notes are all permitted. Remediation is available same-day if needed.

Question 2

What two physiologic targets guide CPR quality when invasive monitoring is in place?

Tap to reveal →

ETCO₂ ≥15–20 mmHg and diastolic blood pressure (DBP) ≥25 mmHg in infants / ≥30 mmHg in children. If targets are not met, reassess compression rate, depth, recoil, and position rather than simply increasing rate.

Question 3

Which drug was newly endorsed in 2025 for refractory paediatric SVT?

Tap to reveal →

IV sotalol — 1 mg/kg IV over 5 minutes — is endorsed for SVT that fails adenosine and a first antiarrhythmic agent. It is a class III antiarrhythmic that prolongs repolarisation. Monitoring for QT prolongation and hypotension during infusion is required.

Question 4

Name two new reversible causes added to the expanded 2025 Hs & Ts list.

Tap to reveal →

Intracranial hypertension (e.g., TBI, CNS infection, shunted hydrocephalus causing Cushing response) and complete (third-degree) heart block are newly included. Both are potentially treatable causes of cardiovascular collapse that should be actively sought during arrest.

Clinical Pearl
“Compress hard, monitor what you can.”

When invasive monitoring is available, let the patient’s physiology guide CPR quality. A DBP of 22 mmHg in an infant tells you more about perfusion than a metronome does. Treat the numbers, not just the algorithm.
