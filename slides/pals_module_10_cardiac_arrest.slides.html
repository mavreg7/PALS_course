Core Algorithm

Two Pathways — One 2-Minute Cycle

Cardiac arrest confirmed  →  Start CPR · 100% O₂ · Apply monitor/defib pads · Obtain IV/IO access  →  Check rhythm every 2 minutes

Non-Shockable Asystole / PEA
1

CPR × 2 min — rate 100–120, depth ≥⅓ AP, full recoil

2

Epinephrine 0.01 mg/kg IV/IO q3–5 min — as early as possible in the first cycle

3

Advanced airway → waveform ETCO₂ · 10 breaths/min during CPR

4

Search & treat reversibles (H’s & T’s including ICH and Heart block)

5

Reassess rhythm every 2 min — continue until ROSC or team decision to terminate

Shockable VF / Pulseless VT
1

Shock #1: 2 J/kg — asynchronous — resume CPR immediately

2

CPR × 2 min — rhythm check → Shock #2: 4 J/kg + Epinephrine 0.01 mg/kg

3

CPR × 2 min — rhythm check → Shock #3+: ≥4 J/kg (max 10) + Amiodarone 5 mg/kg

4

Advanced airway · ETCO₂ · continue epi q3–5 min · treat reversibles

5

Lidocaine 1 mg/kg is an acceptable alternative to amiodarone

🟢 After each shock

Resume compressions immediately — do not pause to check a pulse. Resume CPR for the full 2-minute cycle before the next rhythm assessment.

2025 Update

Physiology-Directed CPR — Measure, Then Act

Diastolic BP (Art Line)
≥ 25 mmHg

Infants  |  Children: ≥ 30 mmHg

If DBP is below target:

Improve compression depth · ensure full recoil · rotate compressor · consider epinephrine earlier in the cycle · check for tamponade or tension PTX

Waveform ETCO₂
≥ 15–20 mmHg

Target during CPR  |  ROSC: sudden rise to > 35 mmHg

ETCO₂ interpretations:

Sudden ↑ → ROSC (check pulse)  ·  Sudden ↓ → arrest recurrence or ETT displacement  ·  Persistently low → poor cardiac output from compressions · avoid hyperventilation

🔴 Critical principle

A 2-minute clock measures time — diastolic BP and ETCO₂ measure perfusion. When both are available, let physiology guide your interventions, not just the clock.

🔴 Never do this

Do NOT pause to check a pulse immediately after a shock. Resume compressions first. Pulse check happens only at the 2-minute rhythm-check pause.

🔵 Pad placement

Anterior-posterior preferred in small infants. Anterior-lateral in older children. Pads must not touch each other. Clear the patient verbally and visually before every shock.

⚠️ Epinephrine concentration trap

IV/IO route: 1:10,000 (0.1 mg/mL) — give 0.1 mL/kg. ET route: 1:1,000 (1 mg/mL) — give 0.1 mL/kg. These two concentrations are not interchangeable. A 10× error is fatal.

🟢 2025 additions

Intracranial hypertension (“ICH”) and complete heart block are now explicitly named reversible causes. ICH: hyperosmolar therapy (hypertonic saline or mannitol) + hyperventilation for herniation. Heart block: atropine + transcutaneous pacing.

2025 Recommendation

ECPR — When ECMO Enters the Algorithm

✅ Reasonable candidates
  • In-hospital paediatric arrest with a reversible cause
  • ECMO expertise, protocols, and trained team immediately available
  • Cardiac surgical populations · myocarditis · refractory VF/pVT
  • Quality CPR maintained pre-cannulation
  • Activate ECMO team within first 15 min if no ROSC
❌ Not recommended
  • Out-of-hospital arrest except in specialised programs
  • Irreversible cause (e.g., severe uncontrolled haemorrhage, devastating TBI)
  • Prolonged no-flow interval without quality bystander CPR
  • Multiple organ failure pre-arrest

⏱️ Timing is everything

The 2025 guidelines emphasise early activation — call the ECMO team at 15 minutes of high-quality CPR without ROSC, not at 30. Outcome data strongly favours shorter CPR-to-cannulation time.

Knowledge Check

Tap each card to reveal the answer

Question

A child in VF gets their 2nd shock (4 J/kg). What drug do you give next and when?

Tap to reveal →

Epinephrine 0.01 mg/kg immediately after the 2nd shock, then resume CPR. Amiodarone 5 mg/kg is added after the 3rd shock, not the 2nd.

Question

ETCO₂ suddenly rises from 12 to 42 mmHg during CPR. What does this mean?

Tap to reveal →

Probable ROSC. A sudden sustained rise in ETCO₂ to > 35 mmHg is the earliest and most reliable indicator of return of spontaneous circulation — often before a pulse is palpable. Pause compressions and check pulse.

Question

Name the two reversible causes added to the H's & T's in the 2025 PALS update.

Tap to reveal →

Intracranial hypertension (ICH) — treat with hyperosmolar therapy · Heart block — treat with atropine and transcutaneous pacing. These are explicit additions to the classic 10-item list.

Question

When should you activate the ECMO team in a child who is not achieving ROSC?

Tap to reveal →

At 15 minutes of high-quality CPR without ROSC in an in-hospital arrest with a reversible cause. 2025 guidelines recommend early activation — outcome data shows worse results when cannulation is delayed past 30–40 min of CPR.

Clinical Pearl

"DBP and ETCO₂ tell you whether CPR is working. Listen to them."

A 2-minute clock measures time — arterial diastolic pressure and waveform ETCO₂ measure perfusion. When both are available, trust physiology over the clock. A child with DBP 14 mmHg and ETCO₂ of 9 needs better compressions, not just a rhythm check.
