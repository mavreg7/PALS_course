⚠️ The Hidden Killer: Poor Compression Quality

Studies show that even in-hospital CPR is frequently below guideline targets. Shallow compressions, excessive ventilation, and long pauses cut survival rates by up to 50%. Quality is the intervention.

⚡ Full Recoil = Non-Negotiable

Lean between compressions. Full chest recoil allows venous return — without it, coronary perfusion pressure collapses.

Technique by Age

The right hands — for the right patient

Infant ≤1 Year

2-Finger or 2-Thumb Encircling

  • 2-Thumb Encircling (2-rescuer preferred) — higher pressures, better survival
  • 2-Finger technique — single rescuer, centre of chest just below nipple line
  • Depth: ≥1.5 inches (4 cm)
  • Allow full recoil between every compression
VS
Child 1y – Puberty

1 or 2 Hands — Heel of Hand

  • 1 or 2 hands on lower half of sternum — not on xiphoid
  • Arms locked, compress straight down with body weight
  • Depth: ≥2 inches (5 cm)
  • Do not exceed 2.4 inches — rib injury risk

🔑 Same Rule for Both

Push hard, push fast, allow full recoil, minimize interruptions. The technique changes — the principles never do.

Ventilation

Breath delivery: enough — but not too much

1

30:2 — Single Rescuer (any age)

30 compressions : 2 breaths. Use head-tilt/chin-lift or jaw thrust. Each breath over 1 second — watch for chest rise only.

2

15:2 — 2-Rescuer Pediatric CPR

15 compressions : 2 breaths. Higher compression fraction vs adult 30:2. The 2nd rescuer delivers breaths while compressor maintains rate.

3

Continuous compressions with advanced airway

Once ETT or supraglottic airway is placed: continuous compressions at 100–120/min + 1 breath every 2–3 seconds (20–30/min). Do not pause compressions for breaths.

4

Avoid hyperventilation — it kills

Excessive ventilation raises intrathoracic pressure → decreases venous return → drops coronary perfusion pressure → worsens survival. Just enough to see chest rise.

Defibrillation

Shockable rhythms: speed is everything

Shockable

VF / Pulseless VT — Shock Doses

  • First shock: 2 J/kg
  • Second shock: 4 J/kg
  • Subsequent: 4–10 J/kg (max adult dose)
  • Resume CPR immediately after shock
  • Target shock-to-CPR resumption < 5 seconds
Technique

Pad Placement

  • Infant <10 kg: Pediatric pads/attenuator if available; anterior-posterior preferred
  • Child ≥10 kg: Standard adult pads, anterior-lateral
  • Pads must not touch — minimum 3 cm separation
  • No gel, no clothing under pads

⚡ No pediatric AED available?

Use adult AED on an infant if nothing else is available. A shock is better than no shock.

Optimising Resuscitation

Chest Compression Fraction — your CPR quality score

0
% CCF Target

% of arrest time compressions are actively delivered. Below 60% is strongly associated with poor outcomes.

What Destroys CCF

  • Long pulse checks (>10 sec)
  • Waiting before/after shock
  • Slow rescuer switches
  • Airway procedures pausing compressions
  • Unnecessary rhythm checks

What Preserves CCF

  • Designated timer for 2-min cycles
  • Compressor switch in <5 seconds
  • IO placed during compressions
  • Drugs given without pausing compressions
  • Defibrillator charged in last 30 sec of CPR cycle

🔄 Switch Every 2 Minutes

Compressor fatigue degrades quality within 1–2 minutes — even when the rescuer doesn't feel tired. Rotate on every 2-minute CPR cycle. Designate a timer.

Knowledge Check

Q

Correct compression rate for pediatric CPR?

Tap to reveal →

100–120 compressions per minute. Below 100 reduces cardiac output; above 120 reduces perfusion pressure and increases fatigue.

Q

2-rescuer CPR for a 3-year-old: what ratio?

Tap to reveal →

15:2 — 15 compressions to 2 breaths. Applies to all pediatric patients with 2 rescuers. Single rescuer uses 30:2.

Q

First shock dose for a child in VF?

Tap to reveal →

2 J/kg. Second shock: 4 J/kg. Subsequent: 4–10 J/kg. Resume CPR immediately after shock — don't wait to check rhythm.

Q

Compression depth: infant vs child?

Tap to reveal →

Infant: ≥1.5 inches (4 cm). Child: ≥2 inches (5 cm), not exceeding 2.4 inches. Full recoil required in both.

Clinical Pearl · Module 02

"Good CPR saves lives that good drugs cannot."

Every pharmacological intervention in PALS depends on coronary and cerebral perfusion pressure established by compressions. Without high-quality CPR, epinephrine reaches a non-perfused heart. Push first. Push well. Push without stopping.

Module 02 — Summary

Master BLS before all else

💓 CPR Essentials

  • Rate: 100–120/min — use a feedback device
  • Infant depth: ≥1.5 in · Child depth: ≥2 in
  • Full chest recoil every compression
  • 1-rescuer: 30:2 · 2-rescuer pediatric: 15:2
  • Advanced airway: continuous compressions + 1 breath/2–3 sec

⚡ Defibrillation & CCF

  • First shock VF/pVT: 2 J/kg → 4 J/kg → 4–10 J/kg
  • Resume CPR within 5 seconds of shock
  • CCF target: ≥80% of arrest time
  • Pause < 10 seconds for pulse check
  • Rotate compressor every 2 minutes
