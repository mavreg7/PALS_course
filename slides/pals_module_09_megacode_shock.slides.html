→ Cold Shock → Vasoactives

Recognition, first-hour bundle, vasoactive escalation, debrief.

Scenario Brief

9-Month-Old (8 kg) with Fever and Lethargy

Setting: PICU admit from ED. 48 h of fever, decreased PO, diarrhea ×6 today.

Vitals: HR 192 · RR 60 · BP 68/40 · T 39.2 · SpO₂ 92% RA · Cap refill 5 s · cool feet · lethargic.

Labs: Lactate 4.2 · WBC 22k bands · glucose 58.

Trigger event: After 40 mL/kg fluid, BP 64/36, mottled, hepatomegaly noted → cold-shock pattern persists.

Team Roles

Team Leader drives sepsis bundle, escalates to vasoactive within 60 min.

Airway O₂ via NC/HFNC, monitors for impending failure.

Access 2 IVs / IO if delays; sends labs and cultures.

Medications draws fluid boluses, antibiotics, vasoactive infusion.

Recorder logs times of bolus #1/#2/#3, antibiotics, vasoactive start.

Family Liaison updates caregivers, obtains consent.

Cold vs Warm Shock

Pick the Right Pressor

Cold shock

  • Cap refill ≥3 s, cold extremities
  • Narrow pulse pressure
  • Often infants
  • Epinephrine first-line

Warm shock

  • Flash cap refill, warm extremities
  • Wide pulse pressure, bounding pulses
  • Older children
  • Norepinephrine first-line

Critical Errors

  • Delaying antibiotics > 60 min from recognition.
  • Withholding fluid boluses despite hypoperfusion (when no signs of overload).
  • Failing to recognize fluid overload (rales, hepatomegaly) and continuing boluses.
  • Starting vasoactive without correcting hypoglycemia / hypocalcemia.
  • Wrong epinephrine concentration (IV/IO infusion vs cardiac arrest push).
  • Missing reassessment after each bolus.

Debrief Prompts

Open with the Learners

  • "Walk me through your mental model at the 10-minute mark."
  • "What told you to stop boluses and start a vasoactive?"
  • "When did antibiotics actually get hung — and what slowed you down?"
  • "How did the team reassess after each intervention?"

Clinical Pearl

"Recognize early, treat fast, reassess after every step."

Each pediatric sepsis bundle element saves lives — but only if delivered and reassessed within the first hour.
