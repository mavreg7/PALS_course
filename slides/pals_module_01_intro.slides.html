Orientation

Welcome to PALS 2025 — Pediatric Advanced Life Support.

This module covers what's new in the 2020/2025 AHA guidelines, how this course is structured, and what you will be able to do by the end of day two.

What is PALS?

Pediatric Advanced Life Support

Who it's for

Healthcare providers who respond to pediatric emergencies — nurses, physicians, paramedics, respiratory therapists, and anyone who may manage a critically ill child.

What it teaches

Systematic recognition of respiratory failure, shock, and cardiac arrest in children; team-based resuscitation; and algorithm-driven interventions.

The goal

Improve outcomes by preventing cardiac arrest through early recognition, and by delivering high-quality resuscitation when arrest occurs.

This course

Two-day provider course following the AHA 2020 guidelines. Skills stations, megacode scenarios, written exam, and provider card on completion.

2020/2025 Updates

Key AHA guideline changes you need to know

CPR rate confirmed

100–120 compressions per minute. Faster does not improve outcomes and reduces perfusion pressure between compressions.

Compression depth

At least one-third AP chest diameter. Approximately 1.5 inches (4 cm) in infants, 2 inches (5 cm) in children.

Full recoil mandatory

Allow complete chest recoil between compressions. Leaning on the chest reduces venous return and coronary perfusion.

Minimize interruptions

Pre-shock and post-shock pauses less than 10 seconds. Target CPR fraction >80%.

IO access emphasis

Intraosseous access is now a primary route — not a rescue route — when IV access is delayed in unstable patients.

SpO₂ targets

Target SpO₂ 94–99% during resuscitation. Avoid hyperoxia — titrate oxygen after ROSC.

Course structure

Two-day schedule overview

D1

Day 1 — Didactic + Skills

BLS review, systematic assessment, respiratory emergencies, shock recognition, cardiac rhythms, algorithms. Skills stations: airway, IO access, cardiac monitoring.

D2

Day 2 — Scenarios + Exam

Megacode respiratory case, megacode shock case, cardiac arrest scenarios, post-arrest care, pharmacology review. Written exam and skills sign-off.

✓

Completion

Pass written exam (≥84%) and demonstrate competency in megacode scenarios to receive AHA PALS Provider card valid for 2 years.

Learning objectives

By the end of this course you will be able to:

Recognize emergencies early

Apply the Pediatric Assessment Triangle and primary ABCDE survey to identify respiratory failure, shock, and cardiac arrest before decompensation.

Apply algorithms correctly

Use AHA PALS algorithms for bradycardia, tachycardia, pulseless arrest, and post-cardiac arrest care.

Lead and participate in teams

Demonstrate effective team dynamics — clear roles, closed-loop communication, constructive debriefing.

Perform key skills

High-quality CPR, bag-mask ventilation, cardiac monitoring interpretation, vascular access, medication administration.

Team dynamics

Effective resuscitation is a team sport

Clear roles

Every team member knows their assignment before the code starts. Ambiguity costs seconds.

Closed-loop communication

The sender gives a directive, the receiver confirms receipt, the sender acknowledges. Nothing is assumed.

Constructive intervention

Any team member may voice a safety concern. The team leader must be approachable. Hierarchy does not override safety.

Shared mental model

Everyone knows the working diagnosis, current treatment phase, and what comes next.

Debriefing

After every simulation and every real event — what went well, what to improve, how to improve it.

Workload distribution

The team leader monitors the whole picture. Do not pile tasks onto one person.
