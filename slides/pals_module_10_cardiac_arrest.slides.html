Cardiac Arrest Algorithm

Two Pathways, One Cycle

Physiology-Directed CPR

If You Can Measure It, Use It2025

Diastolic BP via art line

  • Infants: target ≥25 mmHg
  • Children: target ≥30 mmHg
  • If below: improve depth, recoil, switch compressor, consider epi sooner

ETCO₂ via waveform

  • Target ≥15-20 mmHg
  • Sudden ↑ → ROSC
  • Sudden ↓ → arrest recurred or tube displaced
  • Avoid hyperventilation that drops ETCO₂ artificially

ECPR Considerations

When ECMO Becomes an Option

  • 2025 PALS: ECPR is reasonable for in-hospital pediatric arrest with a reversible cause when ECMO expertise, protocols, and resources are immediately available.
  • Activate ECMO team early (within first 15 min) if standard CPR not achieving ROSC.
  • Best outcomes: cardiac surgical / myocarditis populations with quality CPR pre-cannulation.
  • Not recommended for OHCA outside specialized programs.

Clinical Pearl

"DBP and ETCO₂ tell you whether CPR is working — listen to them."

A clock measures cycles. The patient's physiology measures perfusion. Use both, but trust the physiology.

Summary

  • Branch on shockability immediately; continue 2-min cycles.
  • Defib 2 → 4 J/kg, then ≥ 4 J/kg (max 10). Epi 0.01 mg/kg q3-5 min.
  • Physiology-directed CPR: DBP ≥ 25 (infant) / ≥ 30 (child) · ETCO₂ ≥ 15-20.
  • 2025 reversibles add intracranial hypertension and heart block.
  • Consider ECPR for IHCA with reversible cause and program expertise.
