Shock: Compensated to Decompensated — Don’t Wait for Hypotension

Most pediatric shock is missed because blood pressure stays normal until very late. Learn to read perfusion, not just numbers.

♥ Compensated vs Decompensated 🔍 4 Shock Types 💧 Fluid Strategy 💊 Vasoactives 🤎 Sepsis Bundle
Core Concept

BP is the last thing to fall in children

Compensated Shock
  • Normal or borderline blood pressure
  • ↑ Heart rate, narrow pulse pressure
  • Cool peripheries, capillary refill >2 sec
  • Decreased urine output
  • Mildly altered or anxious
  • Still alert and interacting
VS
Decompensated Shock
  • Hypotension (SBP < 70 + 2×age in yrs)
  • Severe tachycardia → bradycardia (ominous)
  • Mottled, cold, cyanotic extremities
  • Profoundly altered LOC
  • Pre-arrest state
  • Requires immediate vasopressors

🚨 Key Insight

Children compensate extremely well and then crash suddenly. A child with tachycardia, cool mottled skin, and poor perfusion is in shock even with a normal BP.

Classification

Match the treatment to the mechanism

Hypovolaemic

Haemorrhage, GI losses, DKA, burns

Most common. Volume is depleted. Treatment: isotonic crystalloid 10–20 mL/kg boluses; blood early in trauma.

Distributive

Septic, anaphylactic, neurogenic

Vasodilation and/or maldistribution. Treatment: fluids + vasopressor (epinephrine/norepinephrine) + treat underlying cause.

Cardiogenic

Myocarditis, CHD, arrhythmia

Pump failure. Treatment: cautious fluid 5–10 mL/kg, inotrope (dobutamine/milrinone), treat dysrhythmia.

Obstructive

Tamponade, tension PTX, PE, ductal-dependent

Obstruction to flow. Treatment: relieve the obstruction first — needle decompression, pericardiocentesis, prostaglandin E1.

Fluid Resuscitation

Bolus → Reassess → Repeat

1

Default: 10–20 mL/kg isotonic crystalloid over 5–10 min

Normal saline or lactated Ringer’s. Push by syringe in critically ill children; bags may be too slow.

2

Reassess after every bolus

Check HR, perfusion, cap refill, mental status, hepatomegaly, and lung sounds. Stop if improvement is inadequate OR signs of overload appear.

3

Modified strategy by aetiology

Cardiogenic: 5–10 mL/kg slowly, watch for pulmonary oedema. DKA: 10 mL/kg slowly (cerebral oedema risk). Trauma: 10 mL/kg pRBC early.

4

Escalate to vasoactives when fluids fail

If HR, BP, and perfusion do not improve after 40–60 mL/kg, or sooner if overload signs appear, start a vasopressor/inotrope.

⚡ Route Matters

Epinephrine and norepinephrine should ideally run centrally (IO or central line). Peripheral extravasation causes tissue necrosis.

Septic Shock Bundle

Recognize → Resuscitate → Antibiotics → Reassess

R

Recognize

Suspected infection + signs of poor perfusion or organ dysfunction. Do not wait for BP to drop.

O₂

O₂ and access

High-flow oxygen. IV/IO access immediately. Consider central access early if vasoactives needed.

Fx

Fluid and cultures

10–20 mL/kg isotonic crystalloid. Blood cultures (don’t delay antibiotics for cultures). Lactate, glucose, full metabolic panel.

Abx

Antibiotics within 1 hour

Broad-spectrum empirical antibiotics. Source control where applicable. Steroids if vasoactive-refractory shock.

Knowledge Check

Tap each card to reveal the answer

Q

A child with tachycardia, cool mottled skin, and normal BP — are they in shock?

Tap to reveal →

Yes — compensated shock. Hypotension is a late sign. Treat perfusion, not just blood pressure.

Q

Which shock type requires relieving an obstruction rather than fluids first?

Tap to reveal →

Obstructive shock — tension pneumothorax, cardiac tamponade, ductal-dependent CHD. Fluids are temporising at best.

Q

Why are smaller, slower fluid boluses used in cardiogenic shock?

Tap to reveal →

The failing heart cannot handle the extra preload. Aggressive fluids worsen pulmonary oedema and cardiac output. Use 5–10 mL/kg slowly and watch for rales/hepatomegaly.

Q

Which vasoactive is first-line for warm distributive (septic) shock?

Tap to reveal →

Norepinephrine — primarily alpha-adrenergic, reverses pathological vasodilation. Epinephrine if cold or low cardiac output component.

Clinical Pearl

“Reassess after every 10–20 mL/kg.”

Pediatric livers and lungs are unforgiving. New rales, hepatomegaly, or increasing work of breathing after a fluid bolus mean stop fluids and start a vasoactive. Don’t drown the heart trying to fill the tank.

Module 05 — Summary

Perfusion first, blood pressure second

🔵 Shock Recognition

  • Hypotension is late — treat perfusion signs
  • Compensated: normal BP, poor perfusion
  • Decompensated: hypotension, bradycardia, pre-arrest
  • 4 types: hypovolaemic, distributive, cardiogenic, obstructive

💧 Management Keys

  • 10–20 mL/kg bolus, reassess after each
  • Cardiogenic/DKA: smaller, slower
  • Obstructive: relieve obstruction first
  • Sepsis ABX within 1 hour
  • Vasoactives when fluids fail or signs of overload
