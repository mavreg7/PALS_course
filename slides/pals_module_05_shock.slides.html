Module 05

Shock Recognition
& Management

Compensated vs decompensated · 4 types · fluid strategy · vasoactives

Objectives

You will be able to

  • Define shock and distinguish compensated from decompensated states.
  • Identify the four shock types: hypovolemic, distributive, cardiogenic, obstructive.
  • Deliver appropriate fluid resuscitation and recognize fluid intolerance.
  • Select vasoactive agents matched to the shock type.
  • Apply the sepsis recognition and bundle.

Compensated vs Decompensated

BP is the Last to Fall

Compensated

  • ↑ HR, normal BP
  • Cool peripheries, ↑ cap refill
  • Narrow pulse pressure
  • Decreased urine output
  • Mildly altered mental status

Decompensated

  • Hypotension (SBP < 70 + 2×age)
  • Severe tachycardia → bradycardia (ominous)
  • Mottled, cold extremities
  • Profoundly altered LOC
  • Approaching arrest

Four Shock Types

Match Treatment to Mechanism

Hypovolemic

Hemorrhage, GI losses, DKA, burns.
Bolus 10-20 mL/kg, reassess.

Distributive

Septic, anaphylactic, neurogenic.
Fluids + epi/norepi + treat cause.

Cardiogenic

Myocarditis, CHD, arrhythmia.
Cautious 5-10 mL/kg, inotrope, treat dysrhythmia.

Obstructive

Tamponade, tension PTX, PE, ductal-dependent CHD.
Relieve obstruction first.

Fluid Resuscitation

Bolus, Reassess, Repeat

  • Default: isotonic crystalloid 10-20 mL/kg over 5-10 min.
  • Reassess HR, perfusion, mental status, hepatomegaly, rales after each bolus.
  • Cardiogenic shock: 5-10 mL/kg slowly, watch for fluid overload.
  • DKA: 10 mL/kg slowly, avoid rapid fluid (cerebral edema risk).
  • Stop boluses and start vasoactives if HR/BP not improving or rales/hepatomegaly appear.
  • Use blood early in trauma (10 mL/kg pRBC).

Septic Shock — First Hour

Recognize · Resuscitate · Antimicrobials

Broad-spectrum antibiotics within first hour. Lactate, blood cultures, source control. Steroids if vasoactive-refractory.

Clinical Pearl

"Reassess after every 10-20 mL/kg."

Pediatric livers and lungs are unforgiving. New rales, hepatomegaly, or rising work of breathing mean stop the bolus and start a vasoactive. Don't drown the heart.

Summary

  • Hypotension is a late sign — act on perfusion and trend.
  • Four shock types: hypovolemic · distributive · cardiogenic · obstructive.
  • Default bolus 10-20 mL/kg isotonic; reassess after each.
  • Cardiogenic/DKA: smaller, slower fluids. Trauma: blood early.
  • Sepsis bundle: O₂, access, fluid, antibiotics ≤ 1 h, vasoactive if persistent.
