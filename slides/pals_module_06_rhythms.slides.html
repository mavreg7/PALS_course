Module 06

Rhythm Recognition

From sinus to asystole — pattern, rate, perfusion, action

Objectives

You will be able to

  • Identify normal pediatric sinus rhythm by age.
  • Recognize bradyarrhythmias including high-grade block.
  • Distinguish SVT from sinus tachycardia.
  • Recognize wide-complex VT and shockable rhythms.
  • Identify pulseless arrest rhythms: VF, pulseless VT, PEA, asystole.

Normal Sinus by Age

Sinus Rhythm Reference

AgeAwake HR
Newborn85-205
Infant100-180
Toddler90-150
School age70-120
Adolescent60-100

Bradyarrhythmias

Sinus Brady · AV Block

Sinus Bradycardia

Slow but regular; treat if signs of compromise.

Complete (3°) AV Block

P-waves and QRS dissociated. Consider pacing.

SVT vs Sinus Tachycardia

Pattern Over Number

Sinus Tachycardia

Variable, P before every QRS, HR usually <220 infant / <180 child, history fits.

Supraventricular Tachycardia

Abrupt onset, fixed rate ≥220 infant / ≥180 child, P-waves abnormal or absent.

Ventricular Tachycardia / VF

Wide & Dangerous

Monomorphic VT

Wide QRS, monomorphic, regular, can be pulseless or with pulse.

Ventricular Fibrillation

Chaotic, no QRS, no pulse. Shock immediately.

Pulseless: Asystole · PEA

Non-Shockable Rhythms

Asystole

Flat line, no electrical activity. Confirm in 2 leads.

PEA

Organized rhythm but no pulse. Treat reversible causes.

Shockable vs Non-Shockable

Branch Point at Every Arrest

Shockable

  • VF
  • Pulseless VT

Defib 2 → 4 J/kg, then ≥4 J/kg (max 10).

Non-Shockable

  • Asystole
  • PEA

Epi 0.01 mg/kg q3-5 min · treat reversibles.

Clinical Pearl

"Treat the patient, then the strip."

A rhythm without perfusion is a clinical state, not an academic question. Check the patient — if no pulse, start CPR; if pulse, ask: stable or unstable?

Summary

  • Sinus rhythm changes by age; know normal awake ranges.
  • Brady with compromise → CPR if HR < 60 · epinephrine · pacing if block.
  • Sinus tach: variable, P-waves, history. SVT: abrupt, fixed, P-waves absent.
  • VT/VF = shockable. Asystole/PEA = non-shockable, reversibles.
