Module 03

Systematic Pediatric Assessment

PAT · ABCDE · Evaluate-Identify-Intervene · age-specific vitals

Objectives

You will be able to

  • Apply the Pediatric Assessment Triangle (PAT) from doorway.
  • Conduct a structured ABCDE primary assessment.
  • Use the Evaluate-Identify-Intervene cycle continuously.
  • Recognize age-specific vital sign ranges and identify abnormal trends.
  • Distinguish among clinical states: stable, distress, failure, shock, arrest.

The Pediatric Assessment Triangle

Hands-Off, 30-Second First Impression

Appearance (TICLS)
Tone · Interactivity · Consolability · Look/gaze · Speech/cry
Work of Breathing
Abnormal sounds · positioning · retractions · nasal flaring
Circulation to Skin
Pallor · mottling · cyanosis · cap refill

Primary Assessment

ABCDE — Methodical & Repeatable

A · Airway

Patent / maintainable / not maintainable. Position, suction, OPA/NPA.

B · Breathing

RR, effort, sounds, SpO2, chest rise. ETCO2 if available.

C · Circulation

HR, BP, pulses (central + peripheral), cap refill, skin, urine output.

D · Disability

AVPU or GCS, pupils, glucose, focal signs.

E · Exposure

Temperature, full skin exam, signs of trauma — then warm.

The Cycle

Evaluate → Identify → Intervene → Repeat

Clinical State Spectrum

From Stable to Arrest

Earlier intervention prevents progression. Use Evaluate-Identify-Intervene every cycle.

Clinical Pearl

"Treat the trend, not the snapshot."

A single set of vitals can mislead. The deteriorating child reveals themselves through changing tone, rising work of breathing, falling cap refill — repeat the assessment cycle continuously.

Summary

Take-Home

  • PAT from doorway — Appearance, Breathing, Circulation to skin.
  • ABCDE primary assessment, then secondary (SAMPLE history + focused exam).
  • Cycle: Evaluate → Identify → Intervene, repeat.
  • Know age-specific normal vitals — abnormal trend prompts escalation.
