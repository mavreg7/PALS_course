Megacode 1

Respiratory Failure
→ Bradycardia → Arrest

Hypoxia-driven decompensation. Team roles, checklist, debrief.

Scenario Brief

3-Year-Old with Severe Asthma Exacerbation

Setting: ED resuscitation room. 14 kg child, 24-hour worsening wheeze and cough; 2 albuterol nebs at home.

Initial vitals: HR 162 · RR 52 · SpO₂ 84% on room air · BP 92/56 · T 38.1 · Cap refill 3 s.

PAT: Tripoding, head-bobbing, retractions, audible wheeze, anxious.

Trigger event: After 5 min of nebs + steroids, child becomes lethargic, SpO₂ drops to 72%, HR falls to 48.

Team Roles

Six-Person Resus Team

Team Leader directs, prioritizes, summarizes, makes definitive calls.

Airway O₂, BVM, suction, prepares advanced airway.

Compressor high-quality CPR; rotates every 2 min.

Monitor / Defib attaches pads, calls rhythm, manages defibrillator.

IV/IO + Meds obtains access, draws and labels meds, double-checks doses.

Recorder writes times, doses, events; calls 2-min cycles aloud.

Expected Actions Timeline

What "Good" Looks Like

1
0:00 · Scene safe, PAT, simultaneous pulse/breath check ≤10 s. Call team. NRB → 100% O₂.
2
0:30 · IV/IO access ×2 attempts. Continuous nebulized albuterol. IV methylprednisolone 2 mg/kg.
3
2:00 · IV magnesium sulfate 25-50 mg/kg over 20 min. ETCO₂ waveform applied.
4
5:00 · Deterioration: lethargy + SpO₂ 72 + HR 48. Begin BVM 1 breath q2-3 s with 2-rescuer technique.
5
5:30 · HR remains 48, signs of poor perfusion → start CPR (HR <60 + compromise).
6
6:00 · Epinephrine 0.01 mg/kg IV/IO (0.14 mg). Prepare ETT 4.5 cuffed.
7
8:00 · ROSC: HR 132, SpO₂ 96%, ETCO₂ 38. Transition to post-arrest care.

Critical Errors

Auto-Fail Behaviors

  • Hyperventilation via BVM (raises intrathoracic pressure, drops perfusion).
  • Delay starting CPR when HR < 60 with poor perfusion.
  • Skipping simultaneous pulse + breath check.
  • Wrong epinephrine dose / wrong concentration (1:10,000 vs 1:1,000).
  • Failure to call out 2-min cycles or change compressors.
  • Closed-loop communication missing → orders unconfirmed.

Debrief Guide

"Plus / Delta" Structure (15 min)

Plus — What worked?

  • Crisp role assignments
  • Recognition before arrest
  • Time-stamped recorder

Delta — What would you change?

  • BVM seal and rate
  • CPR pauses > 10 s
  • Drug check-back missing

Allow learner self-reflection first; ask "what was your mental model?" before correcting.

Clinical Pearl

"In peds, the chest leads — the heart follows."

Most pediatric arrests are respiratory. Fix the airway and oxygenation early and the bradycardia often reverses without ever needing a shock.
