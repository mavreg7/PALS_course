Module 07

Brady & Tachy Algorithms

Atropine · pacing · adenosine · cardioversion · amiodarone · IV sotalol update

Objectives

You will be able to

  • Apply the pediatric bradycardia algorithm with cardiopulmonary compromise.
  • Treat stable and unstable tachycardia with appropriate drugs and energy.
  • Calculate epinephrine, atropine, adenosine, amiodarone, procainamide, IV sotalol doses.
  • Recognize when to cardiovert versus defibrillate.

Bradycardia Algorithm

HR < 60 with Cardiopulmonary Compromise → CPR

Tachycardia — Stable

Treat with Pharmacology

Narrow (SVT)

  • Vagal maneuvers (ice to face / Valsalva)
  • Adenosine 0.1 mg/kg rapid IV push (max 6 mg) → 0.2 mg/kg (max 12 mg)
  • If refractory: amiodarone 5 mg/kg over 20-60 min OR procainamide 15 mg/kg over 30-60 min
  • IV sotalol 1 mg/kg over 5 min — endorsed 2025 for refractory SVT 2025

Wide regular (likely VT)

  • Expert consult; treat as VT
  • Amiodarone 5 mg/kg over 20-60 min, OR
  • Procainamide 15 mg/kg over 30-60 min
  • Avoid combining amiodarone + procainamide
  • Polymorphic / torsades → magnesium 25-50 mg/kg, defibrillate if pulseless

Adenosine Technique

Fast Push, Fast Flush

  • Use the largest, most proximal IV; central preferred if available.
  • Stopcock with adenosine + 10 mL flush; both syringes preloaded.
  • Push adenosine rapidly, immediately flush with saline, raise arm.
  • Brief asystole expected — record continuous rhythm strip.
  • 1st dose 0.1 mg/kg (max 6 mg). 2nd dose 0.2 mg/kg (max 12 mg).

Energy Selection

Cardiovert vs Defibrillate

Synchronized Cardioversion

  • Unstable SVT or VT with pulse
  • Start 0.5-1 J/kg → 2 J/kg
  • Sedate if time permits

Defibrillation

  • VF, pulseless VT, polymorphic VT
  • First shock 2 J/kg, then 4 J/kg
  • Subsequent shocks ≥ 4 J/kg, max 10 J/kg
  • Resume CPR immediately for 2 min

Clinical Pearl

"Find the cause, then chase the rhythm."

Sinus tachycardia from fever or hypovolemia won't respond to adenosine. Bradycardia from hypoxia won't respond to atropine. Always treat the cause first.

Summary

  • Brady with compromise: O2, support, CPR if HR < 60 + poor perfusion, epi q3-5 min, atropine for vagal/block, pace for high-grade.
  • Unstable tach: cardiovert (0.5-1 → 2 J/kg) or defib (2 → 4 J/kg).
  • Stable SVT: vagal → adenosine → amiodarone/procainamide → IV sotalol (2025).
  • Stable VT: amiodarone or procainamide (not together).
